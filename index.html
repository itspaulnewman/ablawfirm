What is Constructive Dismissal?

Constructive dismissal refers to a situation where an employer unilaterally alters the terms of employment to such an extent that the employee feels compelled to resign. This can include:

  • Significant Reduction in Salary or Benefits: A drastic cut in pay or benefits without consent.
  • Demotion or Change in Job Duties: Being demoted or having responsibilities significantly altered without agreement.
  • Hostile Work Environment: Allowing bullying, harassment, or discrimination that creates an intolerable work atmosphere.
  • Change in Work Location or Schedule: Requiring an employee to work in a less favorable location or altering their hours without consent.

Key Takeaways

  • Constructive dismissal occurs when an employer makes significant changes to employment terms without consent.
  • Employees facing constructive dismissal have legal protections under Alberta's Employment Standards Code and Human Rights Act.
  • Seeking legal advice promptly is crucial for those who believe they have been constructively dismissed.

Legal Framework Surrounding Constructive Dismissal

In Edmonton, constructive dismissal is governed by various laws, including:

  • Alberta Employment Standards Code: Protects employees from unfair treatment and outlines their rights.
  • Alberta Human Rights Act: Ensures employees are free from discrimination and harassment in the workplace.
  • Common Law: Provides additional protections against significant unilateral changes by employers.

Steps to Take If You Believe You Are Experiencing Constructive Dismissal

If you suspect that you are facing constructive dismissal, consider the following steps:

  1. Document EverythingKeep detailed records of any changes to your job duties, salary, or work environment. This documentation will be crucial if you decide to pursue a claim.
  2. Seek Legal AdviceConsult with an employment lawyer who specializes in constructive dismissal cases. They can help assess your situation and guide you on the best course of action.
  3. Evaluate Your OptionsConsider whether it is feasible to remain in your current position or if resigning is the best option for your well-being.
  4. File a ClaimIf you choose to resign due to constructive dismissal, you may be entitled to compensation. Your lawyer can assist you in filing a claim for damages.
